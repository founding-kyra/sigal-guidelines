Never Do

✕No Recoloring

Never apply unapproved colors — no gradients, green, red, or anything outside the 4-color palette — to the logo.

✕No Stretching

Never distort, skew, or resize disproportionately. Always scale uniformly. Hold Shift in design tools.

✕No Effects

No drop shadows, glows, emboss, bevel, or filter effects of any kind applied to the logo or wordmark.

✕No Low-Contrast Pairings

Never place gold on cream or light yellow. Ensure clear contrast between logo color and background at all times.

✕No Rearranging

Do not rearrange or resize individual elements within a lockup. Use the approved files as-is.

✕No Outlines on Filled Mark

The icon mark is a solid filled shape. Do not add strokes or convert it to an outline — use the designated outline lockup instead.

Typography Rules

✓Syne Bold or Extra Bold (700/800) for all headings and display copy — never Syne Regular.
✓IBM Plex Mono for all contact details, metadata, captions, file paths, and spec labels — it gives the brand a technical, precise edge.
✓The "DEVELOPMENT" wordmark tag uses Montserrat Semi-Bold (600) with 250+ letter-spacing, uppercase.
✓Minimum body size 14px / 0.875rem for both digital and print readability.
✕Do not use Syne for body paragraph copy — it is a display font. Body text uses Montserrat 400.
✕Do not use IBM Plex Mono for headlines or long-form body copy — it is for short-form labels, data, and identifiers only.
✕Do not introduce decorative, script, condensed, or other typefaces outside this three-font system.
04 — Brand Usage

Do's & Don'ts

These rules govern how the brand appears across every surface — digital, print, and environmental.

DO Always

✓Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the cap height of the "S" in SIGAL on all sides.
✓Use high-resolution files (PNG 2× or SVG). Never screenshot or screen-capture the logo.
✓Navy as the dominant color in layouts. Gold is an accent — not a background fill.
✓Syne Bold for all headlines. Consistency is the point — own it.
✓IBM Plex Mono for data and contact details — it reinforces the precise, technical, construction-industry tone.
✓Lead with outcomes in messaging — budget saved, timeline protected, mission delivered.
✓Use gold sparingly — CTAs, underlines, borders, key callouts. Strategic, not decorative.

DON'T Never

✕Never recreate the logo in type. Always use the exported files from the approved asset set.
✕Never use generic stock photography — staged smiling, coastal cities, or overly polished corporate imagery.
✕Never use thin or light font weights. The brand is bold and confident — not delicate.
✕Never use gold as a large page background. It is overpowering and creates readability issues.
✕Never center-align body paragraphs. Center is for short headlines and callouts only.
✕Never add competing visual elements — busy icons or patterns that fight with the bridge mark.
✕Never lead with jargon. "Owner's rep" is a supporting detail — not the headline hook.

06 — Voice & Tone

Voice & Tone

Sigal Development speaks like a fractional COO — decisive, mission-aligned, and allergic to bureaucratic filler. Pittsburgh-earned confidence, not corporate polish.

🏗️

Authoritative, Not Arrogant

Say this
"We've guided over $X in nonprofit construction across Pittsburgh. Here's what that means for your project."
Not this
"We are the premier leading experts in best-in-class owner's representation services."
🤝

Mission-Aligned, Not Salesy

Say this
"Your capital is for your mission — not construction surprises. We protect both."
Not this
"Partner with us today to unlock the full potential of your development opportunities!"
📋

Specific, Not Vague

Say this
"City Mission saved $1M. Orphans of the Storm stayed on schedule. Pittsburgh Zoo cut carrying costs by $15K/month."
Not this
"We help nonprofits achieve their development goals efficiently and effectively."
🔑

Plain, Not Jargon-Heavy

Say this
"We handle the contractor. You focus on your programs."
Not this
"Leveraging our preconstruction deliverables to optimize stakeholder-driven procurement outcomes."
🏙️

Pittsburgh-Proud, Not Generic

Say this
"Built on 30 years of Pittsburgh construction relationships — before Sigal Development had a name."
Not this
"We serve clients across the mid-Atlantic and Appalachian region."
💡

Confident, Not Defensive

Say this
"Owner's rep is the difference between a project that serves your community and one that drains it."
Not this
"You might be wondering if you really need an owner's representative. Let us explain why some consider it."
